Circular No. 2520

CENTRAL BANK'S RATES OF INTEREST ON ADVANCES

Commercial banks are hereby informed that with effect from April 10, 1990, the rates of interest charged on advances granted by the Central Bank will be as follows:

2. The rate of interest on advances by the Central Bank to commercial banks secured by the pledge of (a) Government and Government guaranteed securities and (b) usance promissory notes relating to commercial and production loans will remain unchanged at 14 pa-centum per annum.

3. The rates of interest on Central Bank's refinance loans to commercial banks granted under the Export Credit Refinance Scheme will also remain unchanged at 11 per centum per annum for Category I and 6 per centum per annum for Category II. Margins permitted to the commercial banks under the above stated two categories will be 3 per centum per annum and 3.8 per centum per annum respectively.

4. The rate of interest charged by the Central Bank on refinance loans granted for the cultivation of approved crops will be 4 1/2 per centum per annum and the margin permit­ted to the commercial banks will be 7 1/2 per centum per annum.

5. The rate of interest charged by the Central Bank for the purchase and storage of commodities under the Guaranteed Price Scheme and the Floor Price Scheme will remain unchanged at 3 per centum per annum.

6. Circular Nos. 1452 of December 30, 1985 and 2310 of June 15, 1989 are hereby withdrawn.

Circular No. 2599

MARGIN REQUIREMENTS AGAINST LETTERS OF CREDIT

The attention of commercial banks is invited to Paragraph 4 (a) of our Circular No. 2311 of June 15,1989, the validity period of which was extended by Circular No. 2417 of November 14,1989. : ^ : v j . .

Commercial banks are hereby informed that Letters of Credit opened for the impor­tation of goods for sale at the Duty Free Shopping Complex at the Colombo International Airport are exempted from the 100% cash margin requirement, provided the following procedures are followed.

(i) The Letters of Credit should have the notation "Imports for sale at the duty free shop - Colombo International Airport only" (Copies of individual Letters of Credit must be forwarded to the Chief Accountant of the Central Bank of Sri Lanka for purposes of record at the time of opening of Letter of Credit) (ii) The Custom authorities shall, on receipt of the full consignment as indicated in the Letter of Credit, bond such goods for release to the duty free shops only. (iii) The duty free shops shall furnish to the Chief Accountant of the Central Bank of Sri Lanka, every month end, a statement of physical stocks (including those in bonded warehouses) of items subject to the 100% cash margin requirement The statement duly certified by Customs should indicate the opening balance, the amount imported under Letters of Credit (Letter of Credit-wise breakdown to be included), sales during the month and the closing stock at the end of the month. (iv) Persons designated by the Central Bank be permitted to make inspection of the shops to verify the stocks and the books maintained by the duty free shops.

You are requested to comply with the requirements referred to at (i) above on opening of Letters of Credit and also obtain an undertaking from the importers of duty free items for sale at the Colombo International Airport confirming their compliance to requirements at (iii) and (iv) above.

H.N.S. Karunatilake Governor HA. Dharmabandu Chief Accountant

Circular No. 2615

DAILY EXCHANGE RATE

Commercial banks are hereby informed that the Central Bank of Sri Lanka will introduce a new system of announcing the exchange rate for the US Dollar with effect from Monday, August 20,1990. The exchange rate established in the morning, under the new system, may be subject to revision from time to time between 9.00 a.m. and 3.00 p.m. on each working day.

Commercial banks are therefore advised to obtain from the Central Bank the Sri Lanka Rupee rate of exchange for the US Dollar at the time of entering into individual transactions with the Central Bank.

Circular No. 2657

RATE OF EXCHANGE FOR THE PURCHASE OF KUWAITI DINARS

1. Central Bank has decided that Rupee value of Kuwaiti Dinars purchased by the commercial banks in Sri Lanka will be reimbursed by the Central Bank on the following terms and conditions: A. A Person presenting Kuwaiti Dinars for encashment, should furnish to the com­mercial bank, the following:

(i) A letter issued by the Ministry of Labour and Vocational Training to the effect that the person concerned has been employed in Kuwait for a period not less than six months and has returned to Sri Lanka on or after August 02,1990. (ii) The Passport should carry an endorsement to the effect that the person concerned has embarked from a sea port, airport or any other exit point in a Middle East country on or after August 02,1990 and disembarked on that date or thereafter at an airport or sea port in Sri Lanka. (iii) The amount of Kuwaiti* Dinars brought to Sri Lanka should be recorded in the Passport at the port of arrival in Sri Lanka by the Sri Lanka Customs. B. (i) Commercial banks may encash up to a maximum of Sri Lanka Rs. 10,000/-

equivalent of Kuwaiti Dinars converted at the rate of exchange for the Kuwaiti Dinars announced by the Central Bank from time to time, (ii) Commercial banks are further required to endorse on: the Passport, under the signature of an authorised official, the amount of Kuwaiti Dinars en­cashed by the person concerned.

2. Commercial banks may claim reimbursement on their encashment of Kuwaiti Dinars, oh forms as per attached specimen. The reimbursement claim should be made in duplicate and lodged with the Currency Department of the Central Bank, which will credit the account of the commercial bank with the Rupee amount in question. Commercial banks should ensure that the following particulars are carefully noted in the reimbursement claim.

(a) Name of Person, Passport number and National Identity number, (b) Date of arrival in Sri Lanka;;

'(c) Amount declared to Customs in Kuwaiti Dinars;

(d) Amount of Kuwaiti Dinars encashed by the commercial bank.
