Circular No. 379 Central Bank of Ceylon, P. O. Box 590, Colombo 1. 1979.05.02

Guidelines for the provision of Bank Finance for GCEC Enterprises

Any commercial bank in Sri Lanka may provide banking facilities, in accor­

dance with the guidelines given below, to enterprises with which the Greater Colombo

Economic Commission has entered into agreement, under Section 17 of the Greater

Colombo Economic Commission Law (GCEC enterprises). It is not necessary for a

commercial bank to have a branch located in an Investment Promotion Zone for

providing such banking facilities.

Guidelines

1. In providing rupee finance to GCEC enterprises, banks should bear in mind the stipulation contained in regulation 1(d) dated 25th October, 1978, which reads as follows :

"Every aggreement entered into with an enterprise under Section 17 of

the Greater Colombo Economic Commission Law, No. 4 of 1978, shall

specify that all working capital of the enterprise shall be met by

convertible foreign currency remittances to Sri Lanka save as otherwise

exempted by the Commission in consultation with the Ministry of Finance

and Planning".

2. Loans and advances which may be granted by a commercial bank to GCEC

enterprises should not exceed, in the aggregate two per centum of its total loans and

advances, as at 31st December, 1978. However, this limit may be exceeded by an

amount which is not in excess of twenty per centum of the increase of deposits of the

commercial bank over the level of its deposits as at 31st December, 1978.

3. A bank should maintain separate books of accounts in respect of all its

transactions with GCEC enterprises.

4. As it is generally expected that GCEC enterprises should use foreign

resources for funding themselves, the extension of rupee finance by banks should be

minimal. Accordingly, Central Bank accommodation may be available to banks

in respect of loans and advances granted by banks to GCEC enterprises on a case-by-

case basis provided such loans and advances have been granted within the terms of

(2) above. Limited swap arrangements will be considered also on a case-by-case

basis.

Sgd. V. S. Subramaniam

Chief Accountant.

Circular No. 569 Central Bank of Ceylon, P. O. Box 590, Colombo 1. March 06, 1981.

To : All Commercial Banks

Guidelines for the Provision of Finance for GCEC Enterprises

The attention of commercial banks is drawn to our circular No. 379 of 2nd

May, 1979, a copy of which is reproduced overleaf.

It should be noted that in terms of paragraph 1 of the Guidelines, rupee

finance to GCEC enterprises can be provided only if such enterprises are exempted

by the GCE Commission, in consultation with the Ministry of Finance and Planning,

from the requirements contained in Regulation 1(d) dated 25th October, 1978.

Accordingly, paragraph 2, 3 and 4 of the Guidelines will apply only to such

GCEC enterprises that have been exempted by the Commission in consultation with

the Ministry of Finance and Planning.

It has been brought to our notice that certain commercial banks have not

complied with these requirements. Commercial banks are hereby instructed to

adhere strictly to these requirements.

U. A. de Sib/a W. Rasaputram

Chief Accountant. Governor.

Circular No. 597 Banking Department,

Central Bank of Ceylon, Colombo.

May 11, 1981.

To : All Commercial Banks

Commercial Bank Credit

With immediate effect, every commercial bank is requested

(a) to ensure that the total amount of its advances to the private sector during

the period May 12,1981 to May 25,1981 does not exceed the total amount

of such advances granted and outstanding on May 11, 1981 ;

and

(b) to ensure that the total amount of its advances to the private sector granted

and outstanding on and after May 26, 1981 does not exceed the total

amount of such advances granted and outstanding on March 31, 1981.

( m )

2. For the purposes of this Circular—

(a) the term ' advances ' means the following rupee assets :—

(i) loans;

(ii) overdrafts ;

(iii) import bills financed and (iv) local bills financed ;

and

(b) the term 'private sector' includes government corporations and statutory bodies, but does not include

(i) the Development Finance Corporation of Ceylon

(ii) the National Development Bank of Sri Lanka

(iii) the National Savings Bank

and

(iv) the State Mortgage and Investment Bank.

3. Inter-bank transactions in the call money market are not subject to any restrictions by the Central Bank.

H. Premaratne

Chief Accountant.

Circular No. 604

To: All Commercial Banks

Commercial Bank Credit

H. N. S. Karunatilake

Senior Deputy Governor.

Banking Department, Central Bank of Ceylon, Colombo 1. June 2, 1981.

1. It is hereby notified that with effect from Monday, June 15, 1981, commercial banks' total borrowings from the Central Bank at prevailing Bank Rate will not be permitted to exceed Rs. 108,000,000. This amount will be allocated among individual commercial banks on the basis of selected asset items as at the close of business on March 31, 1981, subject to a minimum entitlement of Rs. 1,000,000 for each bank. The limit allocated to each bank will shortly be intimated to it.

2. For the purposes of this Circular, the term 'borrowings from the Central Bank' excludes refinance granted under the Agricultural Credit Scheme, the Guaran­teed Price Scheme, the Floor Price Scheme, the Medium and Long Term Credit Scheme and export refinance granted in terms of limits conveyed to individual banks. These export refinance limits will remain unchanged. Special limits will be fixed for recently established banks.

( iv )

3. The Central Bank is prepared to consider applications from any commercial bank for accommodation in excess of its allocated limit (see paragraph 1 above) to enable it to tide over temporary cash shortages, Such accommodation will be granted at interest rates computed on the following basis :—

(i) First 10 per cent above the allocated limit .... 20 per centum per annum

(ii) Second 10 per cent above the allocated limit.... 21 per centum per annum

(iii) Third 10 per cent above the allocated limit.... 22 per centum per annum

(iv) Fourth 10 per cent above the allocated limit.... 24per centum per annum

(v) Fifth 10 per cent above the allocated limit .... 26 per centum per annum

and

(vi) Any excess over 50 per cent above the alloca­ted limit .... 30 per centum per annum

4. The provisions of paragraph 2 of Circular No. 169 of October 4, 1976 remain in force, i.e., every commercial bank should ensure—

(a) that the total amount of its loans and advances granted for non-essential purposes does not exceed the total amount of its loans and advances granted for such purposes and outstanding on June 30,1976 ; and

(b) that the total amount of its loans and advances granted to non-bank companies and bodies engaged in the business of lending moneys to the public and/or engaged in hire purchase activities does not exceed the total amount of its loans and advances granted to such companies and bodies and outstanding on June 30, 1976, subject to the provisions of Circular No. 289 dated March 27, 1978.

5. Circular No. 597 of May 11, 1981 is hereby withdrawn.

H. Premaratne Warnasena Rasaputram

Chief Accountant. Governor.

Circular No. 636 Central Bank of Ceylon,

P.O. Box 590,

Colombo 1.

August 17,1981.

To: All Commercial Banks Ref: BC/6. Vol.III

Central Bank's Rates of Interest on Advances

(Reference: Section 83 and 87 of the Monetary Law Act).

1. With effect from August 17, 1981 the rate of interest on advances by the

Central Bank to commercial banks secured by the pledge of Government and

Government guaranteed securities shall be 14 per centum per annum.

( v )

2. With effect from August 17, 1981 the rate of interest on advances by the Central Bank to commercial banks secured by the pledge of usance promissory notes relating to commercial and production loans shall be 14 per centum per annum.

3. Notwithstanding paragraphs 1 and 2 above, the rate of interest on the Central Bank's refinance loans to commercial banks granted for the purpose of assis­ting commercial banks to finance exports from Sri Lanka will continue to be 12 per centum per annum.

4. Circular No. 475 of April 21, 1980 is hereby withdrawn.

H. Premaratne Warnasena Rasaputram

Chief Accountant Governor.
